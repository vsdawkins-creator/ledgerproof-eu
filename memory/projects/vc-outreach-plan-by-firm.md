## ⚠️ Email-address honesty note

Most VC firms do **not** publish partner email addresses on their websites. The patterns below are based on standard VC-firm conventions and publicly visible signatures. **Verify each address before sending** via:
1. Their LinkedIn profile (DM as the safer first move)
2. A warm intro through your existing cap table
3. Their team page contact form

When in doubt: **warm intro > LinkedIn DM > verified email > guessed email**.

## Cross-cutting strategic moves (apply to all)

### 1. Lead with the integration angle, not the round
Every conversation in the table above leads with **"here's the portfolio company we'd integrate with"** before "here's our check size." That moves the conversation from "should we invest?" to "should we accelerate this integration?" Different question, different answer.

### 2. The "implementation infrastructure" frame
Every Tier 1 fund has a *narrative they care about* (Responsible AI, European sovereignty, Bitcoin-native, RegTech). Position LedgerProof as **the implementation infrastructure** that turns their narrative into something demonstrable. They don't write the check to back you — they write the check to back **their own thesis becoming verifiable**.

### 3. The Foundation board seat as currency
Hogarth, Hemant, Killeen, Calicott — each could anchor a Foundation board seat. Use the seats deliberately. **Five seats. One per major investor relationship.** The board IS the moat; allocating its seats IS your most powerful relationship currency.

### 4. Reality Defender as the wedge
Every fund Mark Beeston knows is a one-degree connection through Reality Defender's cap table. Use Illuminate as the central hub. After they confirm, Mark becomes your introduction engine to half the targets above.

### 5. The State of AI Report + EU AI Champions wedge
Nathan Benaich (State of AI) and Hemant Taneja (EU AI Champions) are the two most influential AI-policy voices in their respective geographies. Closing either one is **distribution at zero marginal cost**. Closing both is the strongest possible Series A setup.
