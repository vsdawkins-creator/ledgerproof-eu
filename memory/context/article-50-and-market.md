# EU AI Act Article 50 — Honest Market Analysis

## What Article 50 actually requires

### 50(1) — Chatbot transparency
Users interacting with AI chatbots must be informed unless obvious from context.

### 50(2) — Synthetic media marking
Providers MUST ensure AI-generated audio/image/video/text output is **marked in a machine-readable format** and **detectable as artificially generated**.

### 50(4) — Deepfake disclosure
Deployers of deepfakes MUST disclose content has been artificially generated or manipulated.

## Tiered enforcement reality

### Tier 1 — Definitely in scope, audit risk high (Year 1)
~5,000–8,000 entities, of which ~500–1,000 will actively procure compliance in Year 1:
- Foundation model providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, Mistral, Meta, Cohere, AI21) — 15-25 cos
- Regulated industries: banks (DORA + AI Act), insurers, asset managers, pharma, telecoms — 3,000-5,000 entities
- Media: Reuters, BBC, AP, Le Monde, Springer, EBU — ~500
- Government: every EU MS agency procuring AI
- EU-domiciled AI vendors — 1,500-2,500 startups under DSA + AI Act dual scope

### Tier 2 — In scope, low enforcement priority
Mid-market SaaS, EU SMEs (Copilot/Notion users), US enterprises with EU subsidiaries. Will comply via vendor pass-through ("Microsoft says we're compliant"). Year 3 customers, not Year 1.

### Tier 3 — Theoretically in scope, won't be touched
Individual creators, hobbyists, OSS, US-only companies, small businesses outside regulated sectors. Like GDPR 2018-19, the first 24 months target household names.

## Carve-outs Article 50 itself acknowledges

1. **"Obvious from circumstances"** — clearly-labeled chatbots may not need additional disclosure
2. **"Assistive editing"** — spell-check, autocomplete, grammar out of scope (kills half of "AI features" in consumer apps)
3. **"Authorized by law"** — law enforcement, judicial, military carved out
4. **Disproportionate burden** — SME proportionality clause
