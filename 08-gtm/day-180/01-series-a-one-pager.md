## Why this is a Series A, not a seed extension

Three structural facts, all earned in the past six months:

1. **Open protocol as moat.** Competitors cannot enter the open-protocol category without either forking our IETF draft or proposing their own (a 12–18 month process). Closed-protocol vendors are visibly absent from regulator-facing conversations now that Article 50 is enforcing. We are the only credible answer in the category we created.

2. **Foundation-governed, not founder-governed.** The 501(c)(3) is approved and operating with a six-person board, three of whom are independent. The Foundation owns the protocol; LedgerProof Inc. is one of multiple operators that pay Foundation membership fees. This structure passed legal-committee review at three Tier-1 EU institutions during seed-to-Series-A. Procurement no longer asks "what if LedgerProof fails?" because the protocol survives our failure.

3. **Distribution at scale through channels we do not pay.** Big-4 referrals, cloud marketplaces, and FLI's compliance checker (which links to our financial-exposure calculator) deliver pipeline that the seed team did not have. The CAC at Series A is structurally lower than it was at seed because the channels accept the open-protocol framing.

## What we are not asking the Series A to fund

- **A pivot.** The seed thesis worked. The product-market fit is in hand.
- **A second product.** LedgerProof remains a single-protocol, single-purpose company. Adjacent expansion (DORA, MiFID, MAS, US AI EO) is protocol coverage extension, not new product lines.
- **A US enterprise sales motion.** US enterprises will buy through the marketplace channels and through Big-4 referrals. We will not staff a US enterprise sales team until the EU motion is at $50M+ ARR run-rate.

## What this one-pager deliberately does not say

- We do not quote individual customer logos in the public one-pager. Named references happen under NDA in the diligence call.
- We do not quantify the EU AI Act fine pool. The Series A audience already knows the number; quantifying it in our materials reads as urgency-driven rather than traction-driven.
- We do not name our seed investors in the title bar. They appear in the structural narrative ("retain pro-rata rights") because the Series A audience reads that as a signal of seed-investor confidence without us having to make the claim ourselves.
- We do not include a roadmap slide. The Series A audience cares about earned traction, not promised features. Roadmap is for the diligence call.
