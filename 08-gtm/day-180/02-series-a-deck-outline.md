# Series A Deck — Outline and Slide-by-Slide Spec

**Purpose:** The 14-slide deck that follows the [Series A one-pager](01-series-a-one-pager.md) into Series A diligence calls. The deck does not lead with "Article 50" — by December 2026, Article 50 has been enforcing for four months and the urgency framing has converted to traction framing.

**Brand:** Navy #081424 + mint #20E898 + warm cream #FAF7F0; Iowan Old Style display; Inter body. Identical brand system to seed decks; consistency is the signal that the company has stabilized.

**Build cycle:** First-pass deck by Day 165 (mid-November). Refined with first three Series A lead conversations by Day 180.

## Diligence-call pacing

The 14 slides are designed for 45 minutes of speaking time + 30 minutes Q&A. Slide pace:
- Slides 1–4: 8 minutes (set the scene)
- Slides 5–8: 18 minutes (the meat — customers, distribution, moat)
- Slides 9–10: 6 minutes (product + team)
- Slides 11–12: 10 minutes (financials + use of funds)
- Slides 13–14: 3 minutes (the close)

## Common Series A objections and the slide that answers each

| Objection | Answer slide |
|---|---|
| "Article 50 is just one regulation" | Slide 4 (adjacent regulations) |
| "Open protocol means no moat" | Slide 6 (structural moat) |
| "How do you defend against a hyperscaler building this in-house?" | Slide 8 (Foundation governance — hyperscalers cannot own the spec) |
| "What if customers self-host and stop paying?" | Slide 5 (production economics) + footnote that ~5% self-host but pay Foundation membership instead |
| "What's the US story?" | Slide 13 (deliberately not a US enterprise motion at this round) |
| "Why not raise more?" | Slide 11 + 12 (capital efficiency framing) |
| "Why Series A from this lead?" | Founder personal answer; not a slide |

## What this deck does not include

- A "vision" slide
- A team photo collage
- Quotes from analysts or press
- A "go-to-market" slide (the GTM is the customer-funnel slide, not a separate slide)
- A "competition" slide (the moat slide does the job)
- A "moonshot" slide

Series A audiences with the capital we want do not respond to those slides at this stage. They respond to traction, structural defensibility, and disciplined use of capital.
