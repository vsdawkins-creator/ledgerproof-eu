# Asia Expansion Brief — Singapore First, Tokyo Second

**Purpose:** The strategic brief that justifies the Singapore office stand-up funded by Series A. Drafted at Day 180 so the Series A audience sees Asia expansion as a credible 12-month deployment, not a vague "international" promise.

**Owner:** Founder until Asia GM hire; Asia GM from Day 210.

---

## Why Asia, why now

Three regulatory developments make Asia 2027 the right window:

1. **Singapore MAS Veritas / FEAT** — Monetary Authority of Singapore's AI fairness, ethics, accountability, transparency framework is operationally mature. Singapore banks have AI governance committees and budget; they lack a cryptographic evidence layer.
2. **Japan's AI Strategy 2025 + METI guidelines** — Japan is finalizing AI transparency rules that mirror Article 50's structure. Tokyo-headquartered multinationals with EU operations are already procuring Article 50 evidence layers for EU subsidiaries and will extend domestically.
3. **Hong Kong, Korea, Australia regulatory convergence** — Each jurisdiction is publishing AI transparency guidance modeled in part on the EU AI Act. The protocol layer that satisfies EU Article 50 will substantially satisfy all of them.

The Singapore non-profit twin of the LedgerProof Foundation arrives in Q1 2027. That structure must be operational before the Singapore office opens for commercial business.

## What does not change in Asia

The protocol does not fork. Asia-specific mappings are published alongside EU, US, and UK mappings as additional reference documents — but the receipt format is the same, the Bitcoin anchor is the same, the verifier portal is the same. Singapore customers verifying receipts run the same software EU customers run.

The Foundation governance does not fork. The Singapore non-profit is a coordination entity, not a separate governance body. Protocol decisions remain with the Foundation board.

## Cultural adaptation

Asia pilots run the same 30-Day Pilot SOW as EU pilots, with three adaptations:

1. **Governing law clause:** Singapore pilots default to Singapore law; Japan pilots default to Japan law (English-language SOW with Japanese summary cover).
2. **Pricing currency:** SGD and JPY accepted alongside USD.
3. **Reference customer formality:** Singapore and Japan customer references require formal written board-level approval before any logo use; cycle time is 60–90 days versus 30–45 days in EU.

The 4-page MSA-light SOW format holds; the form is not the cultural friction point. The reference and case-study formality is.

## What this brief does not commit to

- **No China expansion in this window.** Different procurement, different sovereignty model, different protocol-vs-platform considerations. Revisit in 2028 only if Foundation governance can credibly accommodate.
- **No India expansion in this window.** AI regulatory framework still emerging; right window is 2028–2029.
- **No US enterprise sales motion before Asia.** The Series A pitch deliberately deprioritizes US enterprise sales until Asia is at $10M+ ARR run-rate; do not let internal pressure invert this sequencing.

## How this brief is used in Series A diligence

The Asia expansion brief is offered to Series A leads as a pre-read for diligence call #2 (after the deck-walkthrough call). Leads who push for accelerated US expansion in place of Asia are showing a thesis mismatch — the Foundation's structural advantages compound faster in Asia than in the US in this window.

The brief is also offered to existing seed investors as a 6-month forward visibility document, allowing them to evaluate pro-rata participation with the same information the new lead receives.
