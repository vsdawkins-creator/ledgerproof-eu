## Long description

LedgerProof turns EU AI Act Article 50 obligations into machine-verifiable, regulator-grade receipts. Every AI-generated output your organization produces — chatbot responses, synthetic media, automated decisions — is hashed, signed, and anchored to Bitcoin mainnet through an open IETF-tracked protocol (`draft-dawkins-scitt-ai-article50`).

When a regulator asks "prove this output was disclosed," your General Counsel hands them a one-page Audit-Ready Compliance Stamp PDF. The regulator verifies it in 10 seconds via a public verifier portal. No vendor lock-in, no proprietary format, no trust required.

**Coverage:**
- EU AI Act Article 50(1), 50(2), 50(4) — full
- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — 24 of 38 Annex A controls (all 8 in A.6 AI System Life Cycle)
- NIST AI RMF — Govern, Map, Measure, Manage
- DORA Article 28 — ICT third-party risk evidence
- MiFID II Article 16 — record-keeping
- GDPR Article 17 — soft-delete semantics with schema-level PII rejection

**What you get:**
- Python + TypeScript SDKs (`pip install ledgerproof`, `npm install @ledgerproof/sdk`)
- LangChain integration (`langchain-ledgerproof`)
- EU operator (Frankfurt) — Fly.io, 99.5% SLO, EU data residency, zero third-country transfer
- Audit-Ready Compliance Stamp PDF generation
- Public verifier portal — any party verifies in seconds
- Foundation Canonical Registry — open dispute resolution
