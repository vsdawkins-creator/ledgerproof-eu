# Shadow AI Inventory — Free 60-Minute Engagement

**Audience:** Chief Risk Officers, Chief Compliance Officers, and General Counsels at Tier-1 EU enterprises
**Format:** One-page PDF, A4 portrait, co-brandable
**Purpose:** The forwardable artifact that opens the GC's CEO conversation. Earns 60 minutes of GC time (30 in the engagement + 30 in the follow-up) where everyone else gets 15.

---

## What it is

A 60-minute structured engagement with the LedgerProof Foundation that produces a **Shadow AI Inventory PDF** the General Counsel can hand to the CEO unchanged.

The PDF maps every AI system known to be deployed in the enterprise, classified by:
- **EU AI Act Article 50 sub-clause** (50(1) chatbot disclosure, 50(2) synthetic media, 50(4) AI-generated text)
- **EU AI Act Annex III high-risk category** (HR, credit scoring, biometric ID, etc.)
- **ISO/IEC 42001 Annex A control mapping**
- **GDPR Article 22 automated-decision-making exposure**
- **DORA Article 28 ICT third-party risk relevance** (for FSI)

Each row in the inventory has a Provider/Deployer classification under Article 25 and a remediation pathway annotated with rough effort estimate.

## What it is not

- Not a sales pitch
- Not a vendor evaluation
- Not a procurement engagement
- Not a paid consulting service

There is no committed follow-up. The General Counsel keeps the PDF whether or not LedgerProof ever does business with the enterprise. The Foundation runs this engagement as part of its public-interest mission.

## What happens in the 60 minutes

| Time | Activity | Participants |
|---|---|---|
| 0–10 min | Enterprise describes what it believes is deployed | GC + any colleagues GC chooses to include |
| 10–30 min | Foundation asks structured discovery questions | Foundation + Enterprise |
| 30–45 min | Foundation walks Enterprise through Article 50 sub-clause and Annex III classification | Foundation leads |
| 45–55 min | Foundation drafts the inventory live; Enterprise validates each row | Joint |
| 55–60 min | Foundation commits to delivering the PDF within 48 business hours | Foundation |

**The Foundation does not see any of the Enterprise's actual AI outputs, training data, model weights, or customer data during this engagement.** All discovery is verbal or describes systems in the abstract.

## What you walk away with

## Why we run this engagement

Three reasons, in order of importance:

1. **Article 50 enforcement is structurally easier on regulators when General Counsels walk in prepared.** A regulator who sees a defensible inventory and a remediation plan is a regulator who is more likely to engage cooperatively. The Foundation's mission includes making the enforcement environment more predictable for everyone.

2. **The engagement surfaces gaps that the General Counsel did not know existed.** Most CROs, CCOs, and GCs discover at least three AI deployments they had not previously catalogued. That discovery is the engagement's primary deliverable.

3. **The Foundation needs a non-commercial way to engage with enterprise leadership.** Selling LedgerProof Inc. through traditional channels works. But the Foundation's credibility depends on having an interface to enterprise leadership that has no commercial outcome attached. This engagement is that interface.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is this confidential?** Yes. The Foundation signs your standard mutual NDA before the engagement. The PDF is yours; the Foundation retains no copy after delivery unless you explicitly authorize retention.

**Who from the Foundation conducts the engagement?** Through Day 90, the engagement is conducted personally by the founding chair (Veronica S. Dawkins). After Day 90, the Foundation Executive Director and trained Foundation staff conduct engagements in addition to the chair.

**Does this engagement require any preparation from us?** No. If you have an existing AI inventory in any format, bringing it shortens the engagement and improves the output, but it is not required.

**Will this engagement become paid in the future?** The Foundation's current commitment is that the Shadow AI Inventory engagement remains free through at least the end of the EU AI Act Article 50 first enforcement window (provisionally 24 months from August 2, 2026). After that window, the Foundation will review whether to continue, modify, or sunset the engagement based on enforcement outcomes.
