## LedgerProof Foundation launches open standard for cryptographic document provenance, anchored on Bitcoin, ahead of EU AI Act Article 50 enforcement

**LOS ANGELES — July 6, 2026** — The **LedgerProof Foundation** today released **LedgerProof Receipt (LPR) version 1.0**, an open, vendor-neutral cryptographic specification for binding the existence, authorship, and modification history of a document or AI-generated artifact to the Bitcoin blockchain in a form any party can verify independently, without trusting the document's holder, software vendor, or any single government.

The release arrives twenty-seven days before the August 2, 2026 enforcement date of **Article 50 of the European Union AI Act**, which requires that AI-generated content be marked in a machine-readable format detectable by downstream verification systems. The European Commission's draft Code of Practice on Article 50 marking explicitly concludes that "no single active marking technique suffices" and calls for a multi-layered approach combining metadata, watermarking, and cryptographic provenance.

"The integrity of the documentary record is no longer self-evident," said **Veronica S. Dawkins**, founder of the LedgerProof Foundation. "Court cases turn on document timestamps. Wire fraud settles in the billions. AI-generated content is now indistinguishable from human-authored work to any party without a cryptographic chain of custody. LPR is one open answer to that condition. It is not the only possible answer, and that is the point. The specification is published under Creative Commons. The reference implementations are MIT-licensed. The verifier is free for anyone to use, anywhere, in perpetuity."

### The Foundation and the operating company

LedgerProof is structured as two entities:

- **LedgerProof Foundation** — a Delaware nonprofit corporation (501(c)(3) status pending) owning the LPR specification, the reference implementations, and the public verifier infrastructure. The Foundation's Founding Board comprises **Veronica S. Dawkins** (Founder), **Frank Gale** of Riot Games, **Pamela Jefferson** of the Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism, **Chris Morton** of JPMorgan Chase Private Equity, and **Kalik [Surname]**. The Foundation does not charge for verification, will not charge for verification, and operates the public verifier as civic infrastructure.

- **LedgerProof, Inc.** — a Delaware for-profit corporation, separately governed under an independent Conflicts Committee, providing commercial managed-anchoring infrastructure, Article 50 compliance bundles, audit-grade SLAs, and vertical-specific products on top of the Foundation's open specification. LedgerProof, Inc. holds a perpetual, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to LPR from the Foundation.

The structure is intentional and not novel. It is the structure used by the Internet Security Research Group (which operates Let's Encrypt) and by the Mozilla Foundation and Mozilla Corporation.

### Standards engagement

The Foundation today submitted an Internet-Draft to the Internet Engineering Task Force (`draft-dawkins-lpr-00`) describing the LPR 1.0 specification for community review. The Foundation has separately contributed LPR to the **VeritasChain Standards Organization** as a candidate Bitcoin-anchor profile for the Verifiable AI Provenance (VAP) framework, and to the **Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity (C2PA)** as an embeddable trust extension. The Foundation does not claim authorship of a single global standard. It claims authorship of one Bitcoin-anchored open profile and commits to working with the international standards community on the larger architecture.
