## The company in one sentence

**LedgerProof, Inc.** is the commercial operating company for an open, Bitcoin-anchored cryptographic-provenance protocol (the **LedgerProof Receipt, or LPR**) that is being released on **July 6, 2026** — twenty-five days ahead of EU AI Act Article 50 enforcement — under the umbrella of the **LedgerProof Foundation**, a Delaware 501(c)(3) (in formation).

## The problem

In 2026, the question *is this document real* — *was it produced when and by whom it claims, has it been altered* — has become unanswerable by inspection. AI-generated content is indistinguishable from human-authored work. Vendor metadata is stripped on every distribution channel. Database timestamps can be rewritten by any custodian with privilege. The cost is paid daily: wire-transfer fraud measured in billions; criminal evidence challenged at appeal; regulatory disclosures contested on the basis of timestamps no third party can verify; journalism whose authorship is contested by parties with adversarial incentives.

EU AI Act Article 50 (effective August 2, 2026) makes this a *regulated* problem. The EU Code of Practice on AI-generated-content marking concludes, in writing, that no single existing technique satisfies the regulation. The market is forming around the gap.

## What we sell

Three product lines, in priority:

1. **B1 — Anchor API.** A clean, paid API any developer integrates in an afternoon. Per-receipt and subscription pricing. Software margins, power-law scaling.
2. **E1 — Article 50 Compliance Pack.** A bundled compliance product (LPR receipts + partner watermarking + partner C2PA writer) for EU-exposed enterprises. Time-bound to the August 2, 2026 enforcement cycle.
3. **D1 — Legal vertical.** Court-grade evidence packets for litigators and law firms, with dual Bitcoin + RFC 3161 timestamp status. Founding customer: Perry Woo (ex-FBI keynote speaker at our May 18 symposium).

## What we are not

We are not the inventor of public-chain anchoring. OpenTimestamps has done this for ten years. We are not a competitor to OpenTimestamps; we are a strict superset. We are not a competitor to the C2PA standard; we are an embeddable trust extension that survives where C2PA dies (when metadata is stripped on social platforms). We are not a competitor to Microsoft's Agent Governance Toolkit; we provide the public-chain anchor it does not. We are not asking investors to bet on a category-defining moat that does not exist; we are asking them to bet on a productized, vendor-neutral, Bitcoin-anchored, regulator-aligned execution of an established cryptographic primitive at the precise moment the regulatory pressure peaks.
