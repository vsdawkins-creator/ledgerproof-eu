### Revised plan (three-fund competitive process)

- Valuation: **$30M post stays anchored** unless one of them aggressively bids up to lock the lead — in which case let it happen
- Close target stays June 25 (don't slip)
- Up to two board observers + one independent advisor (TBD post-close)

### The valuation question

You don't push the valuation up artificially. You let competition do the work. Here's the rule of thumb:

| Scenario | What you do |
|---|---|
| One of them offers $30M post and wants to move fast | Accept it; lock the lead |
| One offers higher ($35M+) to lock the lead | Accept it; lock the lead. Don't push higher. |
| Two or more want to participate at $30M but no clear lead | Open the round to all three; size up to $6–7M |
| All three want lead | Pick the one whose strategic value is highest, signal genuine partnership intent to the other two for co-lead or participation |
| None converts to a term sheet | Re-evaluate timing. Don't lower valuation; instead, lower round size or extend timeline. |

**You do not run an auction.** Bitcoin-native VC is a small community; the funds talk. Running a sloppy competitive process to extract optimal terms damages your reputation and reduces follow-on capacity. Run it as a parallel evaluation where each fund knows the others are looking but the conversation with each is genuine.

## The recommended sequence

### Step 1 — Take all three meetings in their existing slots

Don't try to manipulate timing. The natural sequence (TVP Wed, Stillmark Thu/Fri, Fulgur next week) is fine.

### Step 2 — In each meeting, be candid about the other two

When asked "what other conversations are you having" (and you WILL be asked), the honest answer is:

> "Three Bitcoin-native funds have reached out in the same week — TVP, Stillmark, and Fulgur. I'm taking all three meetings. The round can comfortably accommodate all three if all three want in. I'm not running a competitive process to extract optimal valuation; I'm running parallel evaluations because the signal is genuine cross-fund interest."

This is the truth. State it cleanly. Each fund will respect you more for it than for evasion.

### Step 3 — After all three meetings, write three follow-up emails

Within 48 hours of each meeting, send a 3-paragraph follow-up:
- Paragraph 1: Thank them, summarize what you heard them care about
- Paragraph 2: Concrete next step (data room, technical diligence call, term sheet)
- Paragraph 3: Your timeline expectation

The fund that responds fastest to follow-ups is the one most likely to lead.

### Step 4 — Decision point: June 8

By **June 8** (one week after the EU Article 50 consultation closes), you should have at least one term sheet. If you have:

- **One term sheet** → accept it if terms are reasonable; offer the other two participation
- **Two term sheets** → compare strategic value (not just $); pick the lead, invite the other to co-lead or participate
- **Three term sheets** → pick the lead based on speed + strategic fit, expand round size to $7M, allocate proportionally

### Step 5 — Close June 25

Don't slip. Closed funds enable hiring, which enables the July 6 launch sprint described in `00-MASTER-PLAN/TECH-BUILD-MANIFEST.md`.

- **LedgerProof Inc. owns the commercial operator implementation.** Standard.
- **Patent provisionals** (your 5 USPTO filings) — assigned where? Consult IP counsel; likely assigned to LedgerProof Inc. but with a perpetual royalty-free license to the Foundation.

### Operational

- **Founder equity vesting** — standard 4-year with 1-year cliff; reset the clock to round close

- **Don't agree to consent rights** on protocol changes — Foundation governance handles that
- **Don't agree to a non-compete that prevents you from running LedgerProof EU**
- **Don't agree to information rights that extend to Foundation-only matters**

## Risk: what if all three pass

Real possibility; plan for it.

- **Most likely failure mode:** They all want to do diligence longer than your June 25 close target permits
- **Mitigation:** Have at least one "speed-to-yes" lead identified by June 8. If none, extend close to July 15.
- **If still none by July 1:** Pivot to a hybrid round — combine smaller checks from Ten31, Stillmark, Fulgur, plus angel investors from your existing outreach list (Bitcoin Frontier Fund, Castle Island, Fulgur as participant if not lead, etc.)
- **Worst case:** Self-fund through pilot revenue + family/friend bridge until Q4 2026; delay full launch but maintain protocol momentum.

LedgerProof's runway calculation (without external capital): current burn supports operations through Q4 2026 conservatively, longer if you delay contractor hires. The protocol is shipped and live; capital accelerates GTM, it doesn't enable existence.
