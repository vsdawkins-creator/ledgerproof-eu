## Open questions for the attorney

1. **Existing cap table reconciliation.** Frank Gale owns 3% of LedgerProof. What instrument was that issued under? (Founder shares, advisor shares, prior SAFE?) The attorney must confirm the existing cap table before issuing new SAFEs to ensure the post-money math is correct.
2. **Foundation/Inc. license at the time of the SAFE.** The Foundation does not yet exist as a 501(c)(3); the perpetual royalty-free license from Foundation to Inc. (referenced in the Whitepaper, Bylaws Article VIII, and Manifesto) cannot legally exist until the Foundation is incorporated. The SAFE investors will reasonably ask about the timing. The attorney should advise on how to disclose the planned Foundation/Inc. structure to F&F investors in a way that satisfies disclosure obligations without overcommitting (since the Foundation is not yet incorporated).
3. **Securities law exemption.** F&F rounds are typically structured under Rule 506(b) of Regulation D, requiring all investors be accredited (or fewer than 35 non-accredited and with appropriate disclosures). Attorney to confirm exemption and advise on investor-suitability checks.
4. **State Blue Sky filings.** Confirm which state filings are triggered by the investor mix.
5. **Cap-table cleanup.** Recommended: complete a 409A valuation before the priced seed in October 2026 to support fair-market-value stock-option grants to any hires made between the F&F close and the seed close.
