# LedgerProof Foundation — Bylaws (DRAFT)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Drafting starting point only. Requires licensed-attorney review and finalization before adoption. See `00-NOT-LEGAL-ADVICE-NOTICE.md`.

## ARTICLE I — Name and offices

§1.1 **Name.** The name of the corporation is **LedgerProof Foundation** (the "Foundation").

§1.2 **Principal office.** The principal office of the Foundation shall be located at the address designated by the Board of Directors. The Foundation may have such other offices as the Board may determine.

## ARTICLE II — Purposes

The Foundation is organized and shall be operated exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, as set forth in the Articles of Incorporation.

## ARTICLE III — Membership

§3.1 **No members.** The Foundation shall have no members. All corporate powers shall be exercised by or under the authority of the Board of Directors.

## ARTICLE IV — Board of Directors

§4.1 **General powers.** The business and affairs of the Foundation shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board of Directors.

§4.2 **Number and qualifications.** The Board shall consist of no fewer than three (3) and no more than nine (9) directors. The initial Board shall consist of five (5) directors named in the Articles of Incorporation.

§4.3 **Term.** Each director shall serve a three-year term, with terms staggered so that approximately one-third of the Board is elected each year. A director may be re-elected for unlimited successive terms.

§4.4 **Independence.** A majority of directors shall qualify as independent of the Founder, related parties, and LedgerProof, Inc. (the affiliated commercial entity) within the meaning of IRS public-charity rules.

§4.5 **Election.** Directors shall be elected by the affirmative vote of a majority of the directors then in office.

§4.6 **Removal.** A director may be removed, with or without cause, by the affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the directors then in office.

§4.7 **Vacancies.** Vacancies on the Board shall be filled by the affirmative vote of a majority of the remaining directors.

§4.8 **Meetings.** The Board shall meet at least quarterly. Meetings may be held in person, by telephone, or by video conference.

§4.9 **Quorum.** A majority of the directors then in office shall constitute a quorum.

§4.10 **Action without a meeting.** Any action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of the Board may be taken without a meeting if all directors consent in writing to the action.

§4.11 **Compensation.** Directors shall serve without compensation for their service as directors but may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses.

## ARTICLE V — Officers

§5.1 **Officers.** The officers of the Foundation shall include a Chair, an Executive Director, a Treasurer, and a Secretary. Officers shall be elected by the Board.

§5.2 **Chair.** The Chair shall preside at all meetings of the Board, set the agenda in consultation with the Executive Director, and represent the Board in matters of governance.

§5.3 **Executive Director.** The Executive Director shall manage the day-to-day operations of the Foundation, subject to the direction of the Board.

§5.4 **Treasurer.** The Treasurer shall oversee the financial affairs of the Foundation, including the preparation and review of annual budgets and financial statements.

§5.5 **Secretary.** The Secretary shall maintain the records of the Foundation, including minutes of Board meetings.

## ARTICLE VI — Committees

§6.1 **Standing committees.** The Board may establish standing committees, including but not limited to:

- **Audit Committee** — oversees financial audits and internal controls
- **Governance Committee** — oversees Board composition, evaluation, and ethics
- **Technical Standards Committee** — oversees the LPR specification and the IETF / W3C / ISO engagement
- **Conflicts Committee** — reviews and approves any transaction between the Foundation and LedgerProof, Inc., or any related party

§6.2 **Ad hoc committees.** The Board may establish ad hoc committees as needed.

§6.3 **Conflicts Committee composition.** The Conflicts Committee shall consist exclusively of independent directors. No member of the Conflicts Committee shall be a director, officer, employee, or substantial shareholder of LedgerProof, Inc.

## ARTICLE VII — Conflicts of interest

§7.1 **Policy.** The Foundation shall adopt and maintain a written Conflict of Interest Policy substantially in the form recommended by the IRS in the Form 1023 instructions. A copy of the Policy is attached hereto as Exhibit A.

§7.2 **Annual disclosure.** Each director and officer shall annually disclose in writing any actual or potential conflicts of interest, including any financial interest in LedgerProof, Inc.

§7.3 **Recusal.** A director or officer with a conflict of interest in any matter before the Board shall disclose the conflict and recuse from any vote on the matter.

§7.4 **Founder relationship.** The Founder (Veronica S. Dawkins) is a director of the Foundation and the sole or majority shareholder of LedgerProof, Inc. The Founder shall recuse from all Board votes that approve or modify any transaction, license, grant, or financial flow between the Foundation and LedgerProof, Inc. The Conflicts Committee shall review and approve all such transactions.

## ARTICLE VIII — Foundation / Inc. relationship

§8.1 **Licensing.** The Foundation shall license the LPR specification and any related intellectual property to LedgerProof, Inc. on a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, non-exclusive basis, subject to the Foundation's continued ownership of the underlying IP.

§8.2 **No private benefit.** No transaction between the Foundation and LedgerProof, Inc. shall confer a private benefit on the Founder, any related party, or any disqualified person (within the meaning of IRC §4958) in excess of fair market value.

§8.3 **Approval.** Any transaction between the Foundation and LedgerProof, Inc. shall require the prior written approval of the Conflicts Committee.

§8.4 **Audit.** The Audit Committee shall annually review all transactions between the Foundation and LedgerProof, Inc. and report findings to the full Board.

## ARTICLE IX — Indemnification and insurance

§9.1 **Indemnification.** The Foundation shall indemnify each director, officer, employee, and agent to the fullest extent permitted by Delaware law.

§9.2 **Insurance.** The Foundation shall maintain directors-and-officers liability insurance in such amounts as the Board determines reasonable.

## ARTICLE X — Records and reports

§10.1 **Records.** The Foundation shall maintain accurate books and records of its operations, including minutes of Board meetings, financial records, and a record of all transactions with LedgerProof, Inc.

§10.2 **Annual report.** The Foundation shall publish an annual report describing its activities, finances, and impact. The annual report shall be anchored under the LPR protocol at the time of publication.

§10.3 **Public access.** The Foundation's Articles of Incorporation, Bylaws, Form 990, Form 1023, and Conflict of Interest Policy shall be available to the public upon request, consistent with IRS public-disclosure requirements.

## ARTICLE XI — Dissolution

Upon dissolution of the Foundation, after payment of all liabilities, the remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations qualified under IRC §501(c)(3) and selected by the Board, consistent with the Articles of Incorporation. No assets shall be distributed to any director, officer, employee, or private individual.

## ARTICLE XII — Amendment

These Bylaws may be amended by the affirmative vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the directors then in office at any regular or special meeting, provided that notice of the proposed amendment has been given at least thirty (30) days in advance.
