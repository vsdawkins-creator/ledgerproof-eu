# LedgerProof Foundation — Founding Board Charter (DRAFT)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This Charter is an internal-governance document that complements the Articles of Incorporation and Bylaws. It states the Founding Board's collective commitments and operating norms. Requires attorney review where indicated.

---

## I · Founding statement

We, the undersigned, accept the responsibility of serving as the inaugural Board of Directors of the LedgerProof Foundation. We do so in service of a public good: the continued availability, integrity, and free public verification of cryptographic provenance for documents and AI-generated content in the era when the line between authentic and synthetic record is no longer self-evident.

We accept this responsibility knowing that the Foundation will not, by design, be a commercial enterprise. Its work will be free to the public. Its specifications will be open. Its verification infrastructure will not depend on any single vendor or any single government for its continued operation. The dollars it raises and the time we give will be in service of an open public utility, not a return.

We accept this responsibility for an initial three-year term, renewable.

## III · The Founder's recusal commitment

Veronica S. Dawkins, in her capacity as a Director, hereby commits in writing to the following recusal posture:

1. **Foundation/Inc. transactions.** She shall not vote on any transaction, license, grant, payment, or financial flow between the LedgerProof Foundation and LedgerProof, Inc., or any of its affiliates. Such transactions shall be reviewed and approved by the Conflicts Committee, on which she shall not serve.

2. **Founder compensation.** She shall not vote on her own compensation, in any form, from the Foundation. The Audit Committee shall set and review any compensation paid to the Founder by the Foundation, with reference to comparable nonprofit-executive compensation data.

3. **Foundation IP.** She shall not vote on any transfer, license, or assignment of Foundation-owned intellectual property to herself, to any related party, or to LedgerProof, Inc., except on the recommendation and approval of the Conflicts Committee.

## IV · Operating commitments of the Founding Board

The Founding Board commits to the following operating norms for the first three years:

§4.1 **Meeting cadence.** Quarterly Board meetings (90 minutes minimum), plus annual one-day strategic retreat. Special meetings as needed.

§4.2 **Transparency.** Board minutes shall be circulated to all directors within 14 days of each meeting. An annual public report shall be published and anchored under LPR.

§4.3 **Standards engagement.** The Foundation commits to active engagement with the IETF, W3C, ISO, and NIST processes governing cryptographic-provenance standards. The Technical Standards Committee shall report quarterly on the Foundation's positions in active proceedings.

§4.4 **Free public verification.** The Foundation commits to operating, free of charge, public verification infrastructure under the LPR specification, in perpetuity, for the benefit of any individual or institution wishing to verify a receipt.

§4.5 **Founding Whitepaper.** The Founding Board hereby endorses the *Founding Whitepaper* (`../02-whitepaper/`) and the *Provenance Manifesto* (`../03-manifesto/`) as accurate statements of the Foundation's technical and philosophical posture as of the date of incorporation.

§4.6 **No partisan activity.** The Foundation will not endorse any political candidate or party, will not participate in any electioneering activity, and will limit lobbying activity to that permitted of a public charity under IRC §501(h) or §4911.

## V · The seal under which we sign

By countersigning this Charter, each Founding Director affirms understanding of, and agreement with:

- The mission set forth in the Articles of Incorporation, Article III.
- The independence and recusal requirements of the Bylaws, Article IV §4.4 and Article VII.
- The Founder's recusal commitments stated in Section III above.
- The operating commitments of Section IV.

We accept the duties of care and loyalty owed to the Foundation under Delaware law. We commit to exercising independent judgment in the service of the Foundation's mission. We acknowledge that this responsibility is undertaken for a public purpose and not for personal gain.
