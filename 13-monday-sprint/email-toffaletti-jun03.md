**Brief context.** LedgerProof Protocol (LPR) v1.1 is an open cryptographic protocol that gives AI deployers a way to prove Article 50 compliance through Bitcoin-anchored receipts. Three properties matter for the SME-friendliness conversation specifically:

1. **Permissionless and openly licensed.** The protocol is published as an IETF SCITT-track Internet-Draft. The Foundation owns the spec and the IP; the IP license is perpetual royalty-free irrevocable. Any SME can implement against the spec without paying license fees, ever. No vendor lock-in by design.

2. **Offline-verifiable.** Verification requires only Merkle proof + Ed25519 signature + Bitcoin chain headers. An SME's compliance officer can verify a receipt on their own laptop in disconnected mode. No dependency on any operator service — including ours.

3. **Implementable on €0 budget.** Reference verifier is a single-file HTML page (≤32KB). Python and TypeScript SDKs are MIT-licensed and openly available. An SME engineering team can integrate Article 50 evidence into existing AI pipelines in days, not weeks, without procurement.

EU AI Act Article 50 enforcement begins August 2, 2026 (60 days from this email). The Foundation's strategic concern is that AI compliance regimes systematically favor enterprises with budgets to buy expensive compliance platforms — and disadvantage the 45,000 SMEs DIGITAL SME represents. We've designed LPR to be the opposite: a transparency mechanism that costs SMEs nothing structurally beyond integration time.
