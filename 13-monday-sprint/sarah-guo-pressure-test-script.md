## The 12 pressure-test questions

### Q1 — The Article 50 enforcement-velocity assumption

The Stretch case assumes 14% Founding Member close rate on outbound vs. 8% in Base. That delta is justified by Article 50 enforcement being VISIBLE between Aug 2 and Sep 30 — meaning at least one regulator-side enforcement action that the market reads as "this is real." If enforcement velocity tracks GDPR (18-24 months to visible action), what happens to the Stretch case?

**Listen for**: Does she think the Aug-Sep visible-enforcement assumption is plausible? If she pushes back hard ("never going to happen in 60 days"), Stretch needs to be derisked or pulled.

### Q2 — The cohort conversion rate from Founding Member to Tier-2 ($120K ACV)

The Base case has 30% of Founding Members converting to Tier-2 within 12 months. The Stretch case has 45%. The model has this conversion landing primarily in Q1-Q2 2027. What's a Conviction-portfolio reasonable comparable for 12-month design-partner → mid-market-expansion conversion in regulated B2B SaaS?

**Listen for**: She'll cite specific data from her portfolio. If 45% is way out of range, Stretch breaks. If 30% Base is generous, Base breaks too.

### Q3 — The Big-4 advisory recommendation timing

Stretch assumes the first Big-4 co-authored advisory citing "LP-Conformant" lands by W19 (Oct 6, 2026). That's ~4 months from the W7 (Jul 21) Big-4 working group invitation. Have you seen Big-4 advisory recommendations move in 4 months in your portfolio, or does that usually take 12+ months?

**Listen for**: If she says "12-18 months minimum, never seen 4 months," we delete the Big-4 recommendation line from the H1 2027 revenue stack in Stretch.

### Q4 — The IETF SCITT WG adoption timing

Stretch assumes IETF SCITT WG adoption confirmed by W23 (Nov 3 — IETF 121 Dublin) for `draft-dawkins-scitt-ai-article50-01`. The fallback is CEN-CENELEC JTC 21 NWIP via DIN as alternate standards path. From your view of the IETF / standards-body world, is "adopted within 6 months of first IETF posting" achievable for a working-group-not-individual-submission draft on a sensitive AI-policy topic?

**Listen for**: She may not be an IETF person directly but Conviction's portfolio touches enough of this. If she signals "6 months is fast" we don't change the model; if she signals "you'll be lucky to get adoption in 18 months," we add a derisking line to Stretch.

### Q5 — The Strategic Beta Partner $1M pricing — Riot specifically

The Stretch case includes Riot Games Strategic Beta Partner at $1M (with a $1M discount on a $2M list, accounted as $1M cash per ASC 606). Tencent ownership of Riot creates EU-press risk we're mitigating with counterweight anchors. Two questions: (a) Is $1M Strategic Beta Partner pricing defensible for a pre-launch protocol with no public production deployments? (b) Does the Tencent risk get raised by TVP or Stillmark independently, and how do we frame Riot in the model summary without triggering it?

**Listen for**: (a) Pricing — if she says "way underpriced" we leave it; if "way overpriced," we flag in the model. (b) Tencent — she'll have a sophisticated take on whether to include Riot in the customer-roster cohort visible to investors at all, or whether to defer disclosure to post-close.

### Q6 — The Foundation/Inc. expense allocation

The model splits expenses 60/40 Inc./Foundation in Q3 2026, shifting to 75/25 by Q1 2027 as commercial scales. Will Series A diligence (Qatalyst-led) accept this split or demand restatement? What's the bar?

**Listen for**: She has seen Foundation/Inc. dual-entity structures (Linux Foundation precedent, OpenAI early-stage). If she says "diligence will want every Foundation-related expense pre-audited by Adler & Colvin," we update the Series A diligence pack timeline.

### Q7 — The seed tranche-trigger clause

$10M @ $45M post + $5M tranche-conditional @ $60M post Sep 30 on three milestones (2 FSI design partners + audit memo + IETF or CEN advance). Have you seen tranche-trigger SAFEs of this shape close in 25 days with four co-leads? What's the failure mode?

**Listen for**: She'll either say "totally doable" or "the three-milestone clause is what will hold up the close." If the latter, we may need to renegotiate milestone count down to 2.

### Q8 — The Founding Member outbound capacity

The model assumes Veronica drives outbound through Aug 15 (when EU Enterprise AE hire onboards). Stretch has 25 signed Founding Members by Aug 31; Base has 15. Is 15-25 deals founder-driven in 8 weeks defensible for a pre-launch protocol? If not, the cash recognition curve shifts right by 30-60 days and Stretch's Q4 2026 cash position degrades.

**Listen for**: She knows founder-led sales velocity better than anyone. If she says "10 deals is the absolute ceiling," Stretch breaks.

### Q9 — The DACH press credibility assumption

Stretch assumes Handelsblatt + FAZ coverage drives 30% of German FSI inbound in Sep-Dec 2026. Brunswick (or eq.) gets us coverage. But: does Handelsblatt actually cover EU AI compliance startups at that velocity? Or do they cover incumbents (Allianz, DZ Bank, Deutsche Telekom) on the same topic and skip startups?

**Listen for**: If she says "Handelsblatt covers the incumbents, not the startup," the German-FSI inbound assumption needs to be pulled or routed differently.

### Q10 — The Series A teaser deck timing

Operating model assumes Series A teaser deck ready by Nov 29 (W26), Qatalyst engagement signed same week, term sheets by W27. From your view of Conviction's deal cycle, is Series A signed in 6 weeks from teaser-deck-out for a $30M / $180M post round? Or is the realistic timeline 12-16 weeks?

**Listen for**: If she says 12-16 weeks, the Y2 cash trajectory needs to assume Series A doesn't land until Q2 2027, which means Hercules draw becomes mandatory not optional.

### Q11 — The Big Single Worst Number

If you had to point at ONE number in the Stretch case and say "that's the one TVP or Stillmark will probably push back on hardest," which number would it be? And what's the smallest change that would make it defensible?

**Listen for**: This is the meta-question. Her answer tells us the single highest-priority pre-Wed-send revision.

### Q12 — The "Would You Lead?" Question

If you were leading the seed round at $45M post with the same tranche structure I described, what would your covenants be? What would you NOT cover or carve out from standard YC SAFE language?

**Listen for**: She probably won't lead (Conviction generally leads Series A, not seed). But her answer reveals what TVP/Stillmark will probably ask for. Specifically: pro-rata rights size, information rights cadence, board observer seat, MFN clauses, Founder Friendly Standard SAFE compliance vs. modifications.
