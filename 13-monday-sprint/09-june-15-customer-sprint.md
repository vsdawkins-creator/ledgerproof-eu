# June 15 Customer Acquisition Sprint

**Question pressure-tested through the arsenal:** *How do we get a paying customer by June 15, 2026 — 14 days from today, 10 days before seed close, 21 days before public launch?*

**Why this matters disproportionately.** A single signed paying customer before June 25 inverts the seed-close diligence narrative. The pitch stops being "founder with traction projections" and becomes "company with revenue + named reference." That's worth 30–50% on the seed post-money OR closes any wavering co-lead. The seed-restructure two-stage proposal (Jun 25 + Sep 30 tranche) becomes substantially easier to anchor at $60M for the September tranche if there's one logo on the page by Jun 25.

**This is also the answer to the playbook's #1 unmitigated risk (founder = SPOF) in disguise.** A customer who signs before launch is a customer who is buying the FOUNDER personally — the founder is the brand, the protocol, and the Foundation in their head. That works at 14-day cadence in a way it does not work post-launch when 200 prospects are competing for the same calendar slots.

## The doctrine for these 14 days

1. **We sell what can be bought in 14 days, not the product the playbook contemplates for Day 60.** That means: not a 30-Day Pilot SOW with SOC 2 dependencies, not a Production-tier subscription with full SLA contract grade. We sell the *Founding Member* SKU.

2. **We sell to founder-mode buyers, not procurement-gated ones.** Tier-1 EU bank GCs cannot move in 14 days — their procurement is 90-180 days minimum. The buyers who CAN move are: AI-native scaleup founders, media-company GCs under acute synthetic-media disclosure pressure, mid-market FSI with founder-mode CEOs, and AI-compliance partners (Credo AI, Holistic AI, Vanta) who can buy as a partnership rather than a vendor purchase.

3. **The seed-investor portfolios are the highest-leverage pipeline source.** TVP, Stillmark, Fulgur, and Illuminate collectively have ~200 portfolio companies. ~25 of those use AI in production at scale. ~10 are EU-exposed for Article 50. The warm-intro path from a co-lead partner to a portfolio CEO is 1–3 emails and a 30-minute call, not a 14-day cold-outbound cycle.

4. **The press moment at launch is the actual product being sold.** A company that signs by June 15 buys the launch press release. The customer's name + logo + a quote in coverage at FT, Politico EU, The Information, and the founder's letter — published July 6. That has a marketing budget value to the customer of $50K–$200K depending on how aggressively they value tier-1 trade press coverage. We're selling the press moment, not the SaaS.

5. **Lock-in pricing is the second product.** The Founding Member rate gets locked for 36 months. Three years from now at full Production pricing, the customer who signed in June 2026 is paying 50% of what their peer who signed in 2027 is paying.

## Decision principles for the 14-day sprint

1. **No discount below $25K Founding Member Standard.** If a candidate counters with "we'll do $10K," the answer is no. The Founding Member tier is a scarcity SKU. Discounting it destroys the scarcity narrative for the next four candidates.

2. **No deferring the Foundation Advisory Council seat assignment.** The candidate names the executive at signing. If they cannot agree internally on which executive gets the seat, they are not founder-mode enough to close in 14 days; pass.

3. **Founder available for ANY customer call within 24 hours during the sprint window.** This includes Saturday and Sunday Jun 6-7, Jun 13-14. Calendar is open. If we are not willing to weekend-call to close a Founding Member, we are not actually committed to the Jun 15 target.

4. **Public reference rights are non-negotiable.** Every Founding Member signs to be named in the July 6 launch press release with logo + quote + reference rights. A Founding Member who insists on remaining anonymous is buying the wrong product; pass.

5. **The agreement is 4 pages or it is not the right document.** If Cooley's Founding Member Agreement comes back at 8+ pages, push back. The buyer's legal review needs to clear in 3-5 days, not 14. Friction kills 14-day sales cycles.

6. **The warrant is not the load-bearing piece. The press is.** Customers are not signing for the 0.10% warrant. They are signing for the press moment and the Advisory Council seat. The warrant is a sweetener to align long-term interest, not the dealmaker.
