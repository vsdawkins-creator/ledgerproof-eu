## Letter 1 — Frank Gale

Frank —

I am writing to formally invite you to serve as a Founding Director of the **LedgerProof Foundation**, the Delaware nonprofit corporation we will incorporate in connection with the July 6 public launch of the LPR 1.0 cryptographic-provenance specification.

You have been a principal in LedgerProof, Inc. since the beginning, and your conviction has made every prior milestone possible. The Foundation is the structural piece that makes the protocol durable: it owns the specification, operates the public verifier free for the world, and exists for the next half-century rather than the next exit. The Foundation needs a board that can carry that responsibility, and I would like that board to begin with you.

The role:

- Three-year initial term, renewable.
- Quarterly board meetings (90 minutes minimum) and an annual one-day retreat.
- Service on at least one standing committee — I would propose **Audit Committee** as the natural fit given your operating experience at Riot.
- Service without compensation; reasonable expenses reimbursed.
- The full duties of care and loyalty owed under Delaware law to a public-charity board.

A few specific items I want you to see in advance:

1. The Foundation is structured so the Founder (me) is in the minority of voting directors and recuses from every transaction between the Foundation and LedgerProof, Inc. The independence of the board is what makes the Foundation credible to regulators and to the international standards community. Your seat is part of that independence.
2. Riot's potential evaluation of LedgerProof as a vendor (which we have separately discussed) flows entirely through LedgerProof, Inc. and through the Conflicts Committee, on which you would not sit. Your director role at the Foundation does not create a conflict with that evaluation; it simply requires the standard recusal disclosure.
3. The Founding Whitepaper, the LPR 1.0 specification, the Manifesto, and the draft Articles, Bylaws, and Charter are in `~/Documents/LedgerProof-Launch-July6/` for your review.

If you accept, I would ask that you also lend your name to the **Provenance Manifesto** alongside the other founding signatories — the Manifesto is being anchored on Bitcoin at the launch and is the public statement of who is standing up for this work. Your signature there is independent of (and lighter-weight than) your board seat.

Please countersign below and return when you have had the time you need with the materials.

With gratitude,

Veronica

## Letter 2 — Pamela Jefferson

Pamela —

I am writing to formally invite you to serve as a Founding Director of the **LedgerProof Foundation**.

The Foundation's mission is the integrity of the documentary record in an era when that integrity can no longer be assumed. There is no profession on which that mission bears more directly than the one you have spent your career building. The journalism your students will practice in the next decade depends on the existence of public infrastructure that can answer the question *is this document real* — for the source memo a reporter is handed at a kitchen table, the contested email surfaced in a discovery dump, the photograph the wire service is about to publish in the next four minutes. If our work succeeds, it will become a piece of that infrastructure. The Foundation will not succeed without a director who carries the journalism community's perspective into every governance decision the board makes.

The role:

- Three-year initial term, renewable. Quarterly meetings plus an annual retreat. Service without compensation.
- Service on at least one standing committee — I would propose the **Governance Committee** (overseeing board composition and ethics) and chair of a future *Public Communications & Standards Adoption* committee that I anticipate forming in Year 1.
- The introductions you have already offered into the journalism trade press become a natural part of the Foundation's external engagement; the formal role makes that engagement structurally durable rather than personally favored.

Two specific items:

1. The Foundation's standing as a public-interest organization rests on a majority of independent directors. Your role is essential to that majority.
2. The Columbia J-School pilot we discussed — anchoring student journalism with LPR receipts — sits with **LedgerProof, Inc.** (the operating company), not the Foundation. Your director role at the Foundation creates only a standard disclosure obligation; you would recuse from any vote on a Foundation-to-Inc. transaction involving the J-School. The independent Conflicts Committee handles those.

The Founding Whitepaper, Manifesto, and governing documents are in the launch folder. Read at your pace.

If you accept, please also lend your name to the Manifesto. The Manifesto will be anchored on Bitcoin at launch and will carry signatures alongside yours from the cryptographic community, the financial community, and the field whose work this protocol is meant to defend.

With gratitude and unreasonable optimism,
