## The pilot in one sentence

Columbia J-School adopts LedgerProof Receipts to anchor a cryptographic provenance trail on student journalism produced in the Fall 2026 semester, beginning with a defined publication track (proposed: investigative-reporting capstones) and producing, by December 2026, a public corpus of credentialed student journalism whose authorship and timing are independently verifiable on Bitcoin.

## Why Columbia, why now

The integrity of journalism in the AI era is a Columbia question by definition. The institution trains the field. The field has, in the last twenty-four months, arrived at the same condition every other documentary domain has arrived at: synthetic content and contested timestamps now decide outcomes that journalism has historically settled by reputation and shoe-leather. The technical instruments that can address the condition are coming online now. The institution that gets there first defines, for the next generation of working journalists, what *provable* means.

Three current pressures converge:

1. **EU AI Act Article 50 enforcement (August 2, 2026)** will be felt by every newsroom publishing into EU markets. Columbia's students will be reporters at those newsrooms.
2. **AI-generated content flooding** the trust environment in which journalism operates means a journalist's professional credibility increasingly depends on her ability to *prove* her own work, not just produce it.
3. **The C2PA Content Credentials standard**, championed by the AP, BBC, Adobe, Microsoft, and others, has 100% stripping rate on social platforms — meaning by the time a piece of journalism is seen on the platforms where audiences actually encounter it, the Content Credentials manifest is gone. A Bitcoin-anchored receipt — separate from the file, link-resolvable forever — survives that stripping.

## What the pilot would entail

- **Scope:** A defined Fall 2026 publication track — proposed: the investigative-reporting capstones. Approximately 30-80 student-produced pieces over the semester, depending on scope.
- **Integration:** Veronica's team provides a one-click integration with Columbia's existing publication workflow (whether that is Wix, Squarespace, an internal CMS, or PDF distribution). Each piece, at the moment of publication, generates an LPR receipt with the student's authorship signature, the publication timestamp, and a public verifier link that the student can paste into the article itself.
- **Cost during pilot:** Zero. The Foundation's verifier is free; LedgerProof, Inc. waives anchoring fees for the pilot under a published academic-partnership policy.
- **Outputs:**
  - A public corpus, by December 2026, of 30-80 credentialed student journalism pieces with verifiable Bitcoin-anchored receipts
  - A faculty-authored white paper on the pedagogical experience of teaching cryptographic-authorship discipline to early-career journalists
  - A public verifier landing page (`verify.ledgerproofhq.io/columbia-jschool-2026`) showing the corpus
  - Joint speaking opportunities for Columbia faculty at the AP, Reuters Institute (Oxford), and the World Editors Forum

## What Columbia would receive

- Founding-academic-partner standing on the first open Bitcoin-anchored journalism-provenance protocol.
- A teaching asset — the receipts, the corpus, the workflow — for the curriculum of every journalism program watching Columbia.
- A named-institution presence in the press cycle around Article 50 enforcement.
- The ability to truthfully state that Columbia J-School students publish journalism whose authorship and date are cryptographically verifiable.

## What LedgerProof would receive

- A named academic launch partner. The launch press cycle includes a sentence acknowledging Columbia's adoption. Columbia reviews and approves the specific language.
- A growing public corpus of real-world LPR-anchored journalism that demonstrates the protocol's intended use.
- The Tow Center's intellectual engagement on the questions that will shape the next year of the Foundation's work.
