ARTICLE 50 APPLIES · 2 AUGUST 2026

Unacceptable Risk

Banned outright. Social scoring, real-time biometric ID in public spaces, voice toys encouraging dangerous behaviour.

N/A — systems prohibited

High-Risk

Annex II products + Annex III categories (employment, credit, healthcare, critical infrastructure, law enforcement). Conformity assessment required.

Supporting fit — Articles 8/11/12/13/14/17 evidence substrate

Limited Risk

Article 50 transparency obligations. Chatbots, synthetic media, AI-generated text. Applies 2 August 2026.

Primary fit — 5 of 6 sub-obligations in v1.0

Minimal Risk

Spam filters, e-commerce recommenders, game AI. Voluntary codes of conduct only.

N/A — no regulatory trigger

GPAI (separate)

General-purpose models. Provider-side transparency obligations distinct from tier classification.

Conformity assessment paths (Annexes VI & VII)

Annex VI — Internal Assessment / Self-Certification
QMS conforms to Art 17 · technical documentation per Articles 8–15 · design / development / post-market monitoring consistent with documentation.
Annex VII — Third-Party Assessment by Notified Body
Required when: (a) safety component of Annex I products; (b) certain Annex III biometric category-1 systems; or (c) the provider has not applied harmonised technical standards covering all relevant requirements.
The harmonised-standards lever. Annex VII condition (c) is the structural lever. OCPP-AI as a CEN-CENELEC JTC 21 referenced standard would convert Annex VII obligations into Annex VI self-certification — shortening conformity assessment by months per deployer.

GDPR · architectural posture

Three layers, separated by design. GDPR mechanics live at the upper two; evidence integrity at the lowest.

  • Schema layer: Personal data rejected at write time (email/phone/PII regex).
  • Receipt store: GDPR Art 17 erasure operates per-receipt without invalidating the batch anchor.
  • Anchor layer: 36-byte LPR1 ‖ merkle_root_32 payload — cryptographically opaque to underlying content.
