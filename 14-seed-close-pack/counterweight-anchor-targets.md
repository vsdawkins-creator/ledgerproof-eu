## Why this list exists

The Riot Games Strategic Beta Partner deal ($1M, target SOW redline close Jun 18) carries asymmetric press risk because **Tencent is Riot's parent company**. Without a US or EU-domiciled counterweight anchor confirming in the same Jun 1–22 window, the public launch press story Jul 6 becomes:

> *"Chinese state-adjacent capital backs EU AI compliance protocol."*

That single framing collapses the Brussels regulatory narrative we've built — Foundation independence, EU sovereignty alignment, IETF + JTC 21 standards posture, Politico EU AI policy desk coverage — within 48 hours. Brunswick (or equivalent PR firm) cannot un-write that lede.

The counterweight is not optional. It is the single load-bearing condition between Jul 6 being a launch and Jul 6 being a press extinction event.

## Selection criteria

Five filters, all must clear:

1. **Domicile**: US or EU-headquartered. (Disqualifies all Chinese-, Russian-, GCC-state-adjacent capital.)
2. **Article 50 exposure**: Real and self-evident — the buyer's own AI usage is in regulatory scope under Article 50. Not "interested in AI compliance" — actually exposed.
3. **Named-individual access**: There is a specific human at the company we can reach in the next 7 days via warm intro through 1–2 hops. No cold outreach.
4. **Deal-cycle feasibility**: Capable of executing some form of binding intent letter (LOI / MOI / design-partner letter — the precise instrument is negotiable) by **Jun 22, 2026**. 21-day timeline from today.
5. **Press-counterweight strength**: Their logo on the Jul 6 launch press release neutralizes — not just balances — the Tencent association. The reader's eye stops at the counterweight, not at Riot.
